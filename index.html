How I started Building Interfaces

September 25,2024 by Shevar Roulston

My journey into building interfaces started in a high school project which later aroused my curiosity. I wanted to know how the apps and websites I used every day were created, and why some felt easier to use than others. At first, I experimented with simple HTML pages and basic CSS, which encouraged me to learn more about how design and code work together to create experiences that people enjoy using.

After that project in high school, I was left wanting to explore more about webpages, so I went to a university to study computer science and grasped at the oppurtunity to learn and develop my skills in making websites and webpages.

Designing for Accessibility

September 20, 2025 by Shevar Roulston

Graphic design is more than just making things look attractive—it is about creating visual communication that tells a story. Whether it’s a logo, poster, or a social media post, design has the power to capture attention and leave a lasting impression.

I enjoy exploring how color, typography, and layout work together to guide the viewer’s eye and evoke emotions. Good graphic design balances creativity with clarity, ensuring that the message is both beautiful and easy to understand. For me, it’s exciting to transform simple ideas into visuals that connect with people on a deeper level.
